Why this blog

Posted on 2020-01-19

Who am I

Well, I’m a gameplay developer. Writing mostly mobile games in Unity engine for the last few years, but with a wide background - I’ve worked in various technologies, from PHP + MySQL to JEE with Oracle Database in my years before I became full-time gamedeveloper.

Why I’m blogging

I didn’t really ever feel like I had something to teach other programmers. Never have I worked on a big title or any successfull game you heard about, and when I switched from serious IT to gamedev in 2015 I had lot to learn myself, but in time I found the serious IT gave me some unique skills in writing smoother code and planning better game code architecture. So I was the one with magical skills learned in other industry, and that skills prove to be very useful also in gamedev.

  1. I really want to have that place in web where I can post some trick and when talking about it to some other programmer just throw him or her a link. Yes, there are lot of pages with tricks like that, some of them even specializes in Unity tricks or Gamedev good practices, but on my site I can post my knowledge, place it in my context. My place, my rulse.

  2. I am also still learning a lot. And some of my technics may be seen as controversial. I will be happy to discuss them with someone, maybe learn a better way of doing something. I like to be challenged, some things I’ve learn by looking at others code, criticize it and then hear the explanation why someone write things that way, and it was eye-opener. Feel free to ask why that way, when you see I wrote something strange, stupid or controversial.

  3. For fame, of course …

  4. I am planning to leave my comfort zone some time in the close future. I’m not good at shaders or physics programming and I want to have some place to practice. Probably I will tag this posts or place them in some special category, so it wont be mistaken with tips and good practies. I just think maybe I may receive some helping feedback or force myself to better work when I’m writing something destined to be placed online.

  5. Few years ago, in my before-gamedev era I was quite good webdeveloper. Webdev evolved very fast the last few years and my skills became obsolete, but I like to be sure that I can post something online when needed, created some simple page, not became totally disconnected from webdev skills. Well - some hexo blog with premade template is not a lot but it’s more than nothing.

So what I will blog about

You’ll see. I have some posts in mind, most of them are simple Unity tricks, sometimes with explanation how I discovered them and what problem they solve.

But nothing is set in stone. You’ll see. And I’ll see too.

Szymon Zachara
